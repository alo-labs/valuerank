Methodology
How scores are calculated, why cost gets 25%, and what ⊘ means.

Scoring Formula

Score = ((n − rank) / (n − 1)) × 100

For each of 18 dimensions, models with real data are ranked 1–n (best to worst). The formula maps rank 1 → 100 pts, rank n → 0 pts, with linear interpolation in between. Tied ranks receive the average of their tied positions. Missing data (⊘) receives a neutral 50.0 — neither penalizing nor rewarding models for absent benchmarks.

The final ValueRank score is a weighted sum across all 18 dimensions. Cost weight: 25%. This reflects production-scale reality: at 1M tokens/day, the cheapest model ($73 eval cost) saves ~$4,900/run vs. the most expensive ($4,970) — a difference that exceeds the annual salary of an engineer at some organizations.

Key Design Choices

Rank-based (not raw-score-based): Prevents any single benchmark from dominating due to scale differences. AIME 100% and Arena ELO 1500 are not comparable on raw values; ranks are.
Missing data → neutral-50: Models are not penalized for benchmarks that don't exist yet. A ⊘ cell contributes exactly the median score, preserving the direction of the ranking without fabricating data.
SEAL CI band (±2–3 pts): Due to benchmark variance and missing-data uncertainty, scores within 2–3 pts should be treated as statistically tied. Rankings within this band are effectively co-ranked.
Quality sub-score: The 75% non-cost portion, computed as the weighted sum of all 17 non-cost dimensions. Represents pure capability ranking without cost penalty.

FAQ
Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions about how ValueRank works, what the scores mean, and how to interpret results.
Each model's score is a weighted average of 18 normalized dimension scores. Each dimension score is calculated as ((n − rank) / (n − 1)) × 100, where n is the number of models with real data in that dimension. A score of 100 = top of pool; 50 = neutral (assigned to missing ⊘ data); 0 = bottom of pool. Final scores are the weighted sum across all 18 dimensions.
ValueRank is designed for production software engineering teams, not academic benchmarking. At scale, a 10× cost difference between similarly capable models is a real operational constraint — not a footnote. The 25% cost weight reflects that price is a first-class factor in the value equation. You can drag the cost weight slider in the Rankings section to 0% to see a pure quality ranking if budget is no constraint.
⊘ indicates a genuine data gap — the benchmark hasn't been published for that model. Missing cells are treated as neutral-50 (neither penalized nor rewarded), which prevents unfairly docking models for benchmarks they haven't participated in. See the Gaps tab in the Data section for all current ⊘ cells per model.
The overall Score includes all 18 dimensions with cost at 25% and speed at 5%. The Quality Score is recalculated using only the 16 non-cost, non-speed dimensions (renormalized to 100%). It answers: "which model is best at actual tasks?" — independent of price or throughput. The Rankings table shows both; the Quality Rank column lets you sort by quality alone.
